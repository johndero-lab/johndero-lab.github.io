# Dissertation

## Concerted Resilience: Integrative Policy Approaches for Enhancing Food Security and Inclusive Livelihood Transformation in a Post-Aid World

My dissertation, funded by the University of Delaware Doctoral Fellowship for Excellence, is a three-paper study on resilience and integrated social protection in Sub-Saharan Africa.

The research advances a postcolonial, decolonial, and intersectional perspective on welfare governance, challenging colonial legacies in state-centric social protection models. It recognizes locally and informally organized welfare institutions—remittances, community networks, cooperatives, kinship systems—as integral to welfare provision.

---

## Dissertation Papers

**Paper 1: Closing the Hunger Gap** (2026)  
Can integrated social protection systems improve food security outcomes for rural households? A causal impact evaluation using household survey data and difference-in-differences design.

**Paper 2: Closing the Poverty Gap** (2026)  
Does integrated social protection foster inclusive rural transformation? A causal analysis of livelihood diversification among vulnerable households in Nigeria.

**Paper 3: Concerted Resilience** (2026)  
A governance approach to integrating social protection, local institutions, and policy systems for transformative resilience in Sub-Saharan Africa.

---

## Key Insights

- Integrated social protection (combining formal and informal systems) produces stronger resilience outcomes
- Decolonial approaches recognize community institutions as integral, not residual
- Intersectional vulnerability requires multidimensional policy responses
